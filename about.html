About Us

Listing Lab started with a simple observation: resale work gets messy before the listing is ever posted.

Sellers still need to gather item details, check price direction, write clear copy, remember shipping language, and keep track of what should happen next. Listing Lab was built to make that prep stage feel more organized, more repeatable, and easier to manage.

Who It Is For

Listing Lab is for sellers who are doing real listing prep work but do not need a giant backend system just to stay organized. It is aimed at the person who wants a cleaner workspace before they post to eBay, Poshmark, or another marketplace.

That includes students building resale tools, small sellers managing inventory from a laptop, and anyone who wants a practical workflow for pricing, copy, and saved listing records.

Why It Was Built

The product came from the gap between listing research and listing execution. There are plenty of places to post items, but there are fewer tools that help with the prep work leading up to that post.

Listing Lab focuses on that gap. It was built to support the stage where the seller is still thinking through price, structure, wording, and organization before anything goes live.

What We Wanted the Experience to Feel Like

The goal was to make resale prep feel less scattered. Instead of bouncing between notes, pricing tabs, draft captions, and saved screenshots, the user should be able to move through a cleaner sequence inside 1 workspace.

That is why Listing Lab stays focused on practical tasks: gather item details, shape price direction, build buyer-facing copy, save the record, and come back to it later. The product is intentionally local-first and front-end only, which keeps the workflow lightweight while still feeling like a real tool.

Where It Stands Now

Listing Lab is a polished front-end capstone project with a clear concept: help resellers handle the pre-listing stage more effectively. It is not pretending to be a full marketplace platform, and it does not rely on heavy backend infrastructure to demonstrate its value.

What it does show is a focused product direction, a clear seller workflow, and a set of connected features that solve a real organization problem in resale work.
